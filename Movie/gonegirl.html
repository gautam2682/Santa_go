Gone Girl



2014 IMDB-8.1

With his wife's disappearance having become the focus of an intense media circus, a man sees the spotlight turned on him when it's suspected that he may not be innocent.

If you haven’t seen David Fincher’s Gone Girl already, you’re missing out on a truly uncomfortable but otherwise enlightening two and a half hours. Like the novel by Gillian Flynn on which the film is based, Gone Girl with existential gems that will have you rethinking your deductive reasoning, and quite possibly, every romantic relationship you ever thought you had. Beyond the gore and anxiety-driven conflict, Gone Girl is riddled with several important lessons, many of which you’ll probably learn within the first fifteen minutes. Here are lessons you definitly want to learn:-

1. Be With Someone Who Brings Out Your Best

The statement itself says volumes about staying away from the people that have negative influnce on you and be with the people that have googd impact and brings the best in you. Somebody has rightly quoted:-
"Do not be with the best guy but be with the guy which bring the best in you.

2. Keep Your Siblings Close

Anyone would be lucky to have a caring sister, so don’t let your siblings leave your life, because you never know when you’ll need bail money .She’s there for her brother when he’s accused of murder, she doesn’t judge him when he decides to stay with his insane wife, and she even plays childish board games with him in a bar to get his mind off of his troubles

3.Unemployment Isn’t The End Of The World

Losing your job doesn’t mean that you have to lose your partner, too. Amy and Nick’s relationship got a bit rocky after they both lost their jobs, but they were able push past their financial trouble. Nick opened a bar with his sister, and Amy used whatever money she had left from her parents’ book deals. Even if you don’t have a few million hidden away, you can always make it through a period of unemployment. The struggle doesn’t have to ruin your relationship – it can actually make you and your partner stronger as a couple. You just have to remember that you’re on the same team.

3. Don’t Get Lazy

You can’t expect your relationship to be the same after five years as it was when you first met. When you start dating, you’ll have sex in the library, buy each other satin sheets and all the other cliches. You’ll celebrate your anniversary by going on romantic treasure hunts and stripping down to shower together. However, as you grow more comfortable (and bored) with each other, you’ll stop giving out compliments and coming home for dinner. The honeymoon never lasts, but that doesn’t mean that you should give up trying altogether. If you do, it just might end in murder – or at least a divorce.

4.Every Story Has Multiple Sides

Like the novel, the film relies on the concept of an unreliable narrator,. Early on, the audience is asked to make a hypothesis regarding Amy’s “murder” based on little information, and the whole story isn’t revealed until Gone Girl’s conclusion. Throughout the film, the narration flip flops between Nick’s supposed guilt, Amy’s diary, and later, Amy’s life on the run after the audience realizes she faked her own death.

5.The media (and even the Entertainment Industry) has the power to provoke you to like someone ... or dislike someone

What the movie Gone Girl highlights in a subtle manner is that if you are perceived as "likeable" by the general public, people do not really care if you have flaws and weaknesses in your personality, or if you have done a few wrong things in your life. On the flip side, once you are branded as an "unlikeable" person, no one will want to associate with you even if you are truly a good, quality person who generally treats people right behind closed doors.
