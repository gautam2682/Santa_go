THE INTENRSHIP



2013 IMDB-6.3

Salesmen Billy and Nick find themselves unemployed in the digital world. In a bid to prove their competence, they try to bag an internship at Google, where they must beat some tech-savvy geniuses.

Intersteller is definately a movie beyond the horizons of mankind , a movie of its own kind . This movie beautifuly potrays a picture of what can happen in near future supported by facts and figures.A emotional engaging story with eternal love for family. A movie in which at last you will be compelled in the end to search for the things that you can't understand.So here are lessons worth to learn.

1. Learning from your peers can help your career.

The film is centred around a team of interns working together. Each intern has different qualities which enable the team to (eventually) function efficiently. When you are in your internship make sure you take the time to get to know your peers and colleagues so that you can learn from their personal qualities. This counts for both technical and soft skills!

2. Seize the time and oppertunity.

We can have anything later on, we can have love, money, fame etc later on. But there is one thing we’ve to seize now. It’s the time, the opportunity, see there is no difference between time and opportunity. Seize both of these things whenever you get them.

3. It's never too late to switch gears

"The Internship" isn't a movie about Google -- it's about reinvention, according to an interview Vaughn did with Australia's The Age newspaper: "Yes, you get a peek behind the chocolate factory, but it's really about wanting to start over, and I wanted to pick an industry that authentically felt like there was a future there." In real-life, starting over after a layoff may not be quite as dramatic. It might also require using your skills in a new way, focusing on a section of your industry that is growing or transitioning into freelancing.

4. Education will only get you in the door

Early in the movie, the standout online interview seals the deal, rather than the pair's fake degrees from The University of Phoenix. Later on, the better-educated villain spends more time talking trash than working on his projects and doesn't get a job offer. Some internship and job-seekers lament the fact that they didn't get into, or couldn't afford to attend, a top-tier college, and therefore are at a disadvantage. While it's true that the name of a great school on your resume can get you in the door for an interview, the same can be said for a strong network, an excellent cover letter, an eye-catching blog or a strong presence on social media. And once you're in that door, your education is usually only as useful to your success as you make it.

5. Be open to learning

Vaughn and Wilson enter the internship fairly clueless on technical issues (Wilson's character says "C++? I thought that was C+ but with an extra + because it's so good?" Yet they persevere throughout the internship to continue learning new skills by studying in their spare time, using their colleagues’ knowledge to learn from as well as attending extra lectures and learning seminars. Make use of all the learning opportunities at your internship so that you can leave with as much knowledge as possible.

6.keep on working to achieve the great.

GOOD will come infront of you so that you don’t achieve GREAT: While you are in pursuite of your vision, of your destinations, there will come up some other really good stuff with a settlement, and that settlement will not let you achieve the great. In this situation you’ve got to keep yourself on the track you are working on and keep on working to achieve the great. Yeah you will face some real setbacks to get you down, but my dear you gotta keep it on.
