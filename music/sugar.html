SUGAR




2012 Moroon 5

Sugar, like many other songs by Maroon 5, is about love, desires and emotions.

I can sum It begins with Adam Levine (Lead singer) expressing a strong craving for his lover. It is almost like he is in withdrawal and begins to suffer when he isn’t with her. He needs her close and wants her love, warmth and comfort during his difficult moments.

1. Need for the person that you love and can't be without.

He establishes that without his lover, he hurts and is weak, even broken, to the point of begging.I think it perfectly describes that need for the person that you love and can't be without.

2. lover is the meaning of everything .

When he is hurt, broken and weak, he suggests she is able to put him back together. When she's gone, he's insecure because this lover is the meaning of everything for him. This verse also reinforces that he is begging for her attention and affection.

3. Be real person

Through the chorus (which I'll get to in a moment) he constantly refers to her affection as "sugar". Levine has also apparently stated that he is comparing her live to a red velvet cake. I'm not sure if anyone else has had a good red velvet but that stuff is sweet and rich and heavy and heavenly if you have a sugar craving. Much like how really needing a lover's affection can feel sometimes. He is also stating his intent to be the one for her, as in an exclusive relationship, and probably is expressing his sentiment on her physical appearance which is, really, the only time it happens in this song. Then he follows it up with a promise that she doesn't have to be afraid that he's toying with her. He doesn't want her to be shy, and he doesn't want her to be fake. She can be confident that he wants to be with her and no one else.

4.Everyone needs love and sympathy.

"Sugar? Yes, please!" He's enthusiastically indicating he wants the "sugar" or, in this case, her love and affection. And he asks directly for her to give it to him. He also clarifies that he needs her love and sympathy. He's already indicated in verses 1 and 2 that he is weak and broken without her, and that she can pick up his broken pieces. Here, he clarifies it's her love and sympathy (which doesn't exactly indicate physical aspects of their relationship, but that doesn't mean it isn't, either). He reaffirms she shows him good loving (still not necessarily physical) and follows it up with the enthusiastic request for her sugar (love).
The meaning of Sugar is describing being in love like loving candy. Like a sugar-addict needing a sugar-fix, the lyrics say I need it now in all sorts of ways. (It's killing me... begging... I gotta get... You are the one thing I'm living for...) Sound cool? But if love is just filling up a need, you're just an object to the person who says he loves/needs you. And comparing love (or sex) to hunger leaves relationships just biological. So Sugar-love is just a biological need, the same way I really love ice cream. Wait! Is that really love?
